## 1. Purpose

School wishes to run voluntary donation-based fundraising campaigns for School’s programs, and Company provides software, payment processing coordination, and related services described in this Agreement. This Agreement governs the **relationship between Company and School** generally. Each specific campaign may be further described in a **Fundraiser Campaign Addendum** signed by the Parties and incorporated by reference.

## 2. How the fundraiser works (overview)

2.1 **Platform.** Company provides a web and/or mobile experience through which School-designated participants (e.g., coaches, students) may share personal donation links with supporters.

2.2 **Donations.** Supporters make voluntary donations through a third-party payment processor (currently **Stripe** or a successor designated by Company). Donations are **not** tax advice; whether any donation is tax-deductible is **School’s responsibility** to determine and communicate in compliance with law.

2.3 **Campaign dates.** Company may enforce **start and end dates** for texting and/or online donations as configured for each campaign. School is responsible for ensuring campaign dates and communications align with School’s policies and applicable law.

2.4 **No guarantee of results.** Company does not guarantee any level of donations, participation, or revenue.

## 3. Parties and roles

3.1 **School** is responsible for: (a) authorizing the campaign; (b) designating authorized representatives (e.g., coaches, administrators); (c) ensuring participants comply with School policies and law; (d) accurate information provided to Company; (e) tax and legal compliance relating to School’s receipt and use of funds; and (f) communications to donors as required by law or School policy.

3.2 **Company** provides the **technology and operational support** described in Section 4 and does not control School’s programs, messaging to donors (except as built into the product), or School’s use of net proceeds.

## 5. Money flow, ownership of funds, and fees

5.1 **Gross donations.** “**Gross Donations**” means the total amount charged to donors for a campaign, before payment processing fees.

5.2 **Processing fees.** Third-party payment processors (e.g., **Stripe**) charge **processing fees** on transactions. Fees may include percentage and fixed per-transaction components and may change according to the processor’s terms. **School acknowledges that processing fees reduce the amount School receives.**

5.3 **Net proceeds.** Subject to Section 5.4, **School is entitled to the Net Proceeds** of each campaign, defined as **Gross Donations minus processing fees and any other fees disclosed in writing** (including in a Campaign Addendum or fee schedule) **specific to that campaign**. Company does not claim ownership of School’s Net Proceeds except as needed to remit fees owed to Company (if any) as separately agreed in writing.

5.4 **Chargebacks, refunds, and reversals.** Amounts **refunded**, **charged back**, **reversed**, or **clawed back** by a payment processor or donor are **deducted** from amounts otherwise due to School. School is responsible for the economic effect of such reversals.

5.5 **No commingling representation.** Company’s systems may hold funds in transit through Stripe (or successor) according to that provider’s settlement rules. **Legal and tax characterization** of funds in transit should be confirmed by School’s counsel and tax advisors.
